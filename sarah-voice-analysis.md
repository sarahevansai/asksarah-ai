## Teaching Patterns

### How Sarah structures explanations:

**1. The Reveal Pattern (her go-to)**
She starts with an experiment or audit → shows a surprising gap → explains WHY the gap exists → gives the fix. Almost every teaching moment follows this arc:
- "I asked ChatGPT to recommend a company in my client's category. My client wasn't mentioned. Their competitor with 8% market share was listed first."
- Then the breakdown: here's what the competitor had, here's what my client had, here's the structural gap.

**2. The "Old vs. New" Reframe**
She constantly juxtaposes the old world with the new reality:
- "The old funnel: Awareness → Consideration → Decision. The new reality: AI Answer → Maybe Click → Decision."
- "Old metrics are actively misleading" → then she maps old → new
- "What's old is new again" — she also notes that some traditional PR tactics are coming back because of AI

**3. The Timeline Anchor**
She always frames work in concrete phases: 90-day systems, 24-month journeys, 60-day fixes. She never says "this will take a while." She says:
- "Over 24 months, we build to the formula and identify what works for you."
- "We started closing that gap in 60 days."
- "Everything has to be approved in 90-day increments. This is a 90-day system."

**4. The "Try It Right Now" Push**
She turns every concept into an immediate action:
- "Try it today. You might not love what you find."
- "Ask ChatGPT about your brand. Then DM me what you found."
- "Grab a coffee. Open four tabs. Let's go."

**5. The Pain-to-Objective Bridge**
On sales calls, she explicitly aligns on the pain first: "Do you agree with this pain point as the number one thing to focus on to get you there as part of your 24-month journey?" She doesn't skip to solutions until the client has said yes to the problem.

- **Refresh**: What stays current — models retrain, you have to keep feeding the system

### The 90-Day System
