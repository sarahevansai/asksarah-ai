# 4. PR Can Finally Prove ROI (If You Know Where to Look)
**Date:** October 13, 2025

Half the industry admits they cannot prove PR's ROI. That is not a failure of PR, it is a missing data trail.

### The Two Numbers That Matter:

**Prompt Index** — A structured list of real buyer questions you want to "own" across AI engines. Organized by intent layer.

**Answer Share (AS)** — The percentage of AI answers that include your brand for a defined prompt set and intent layer.

**Answer Share Formula:** AS% = (Brand-cited answers ÷ Total answers for a prompt set) × 100

### Intent Layers:

🟢 **Informational:** "What is workforce compliance?"
🟡 **Consideration:** "Top global workforce compliance companies"
🔴 **Conversion:** "Which compliance platform integrates with Workday?"

### How to Build Your Prompt Index (Step by Step):

1. **Seed sources:** pull your sales FAQs, RFPs, competitor pages, product docs, and recent win/loss notes
2. **Draft 1000 prompts:** informational, consideration, conversion — in buyer language, not brand language
3. **Normalize syntax:** remove brand names from informational prompts, include category terms in consideration prompts
4. **De-duplicate and cluster:** group by topic, keep 3–5 variants per topic
5. **Lock v1:** freeze the list for 30 days so you can measure change

### Measurement Protocol:

- **Platforms:** ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity
- **Runs:** 3 passes per model to reduce variance
- **Cadence:** monthly for directional movement, weekly during launches

**Log for each prompt:** Model, date, time, exact prompt text, did your brand appear (Y/N), how cited, citations the model used, competitors mentioned, notes

### Answer Share Targets:

- **Informational AS%:** 20–40% within 90 days for niche B2B categories
- **Consideration AS%:** 10–25% within 90 days with credible third-party validation
- **Conversion AS%:** 5–15% within 90 days (requires integrations, case studies, analyst-style content)

### 5 Plays to Increase Answer Share:

1. **Structured Coverage** — Pitch listicles, comparisons, and buyer-guide formats
2. **Analyst-Style Assets** — Publish definition → insight → proof → implication briefs
3. **Answer-Ready Thought Leadership** — Author posts that directly answer your top prompts
4. **Source Hygiene** — Standardize product pages with clear H2s, comparison tables, and last-updated stamps
5. **Third-Party Proofs** — Secure quotes in trade pubs, customer stories, and rankings

### 90-Day Implementation Plan:

**Day 1–30:** Build Prompt Index v1, baseline Answer Share, identify top 20 cited domains and gaps
**Day 31–60:** Ship 4 answer-ready assets, pitch 6 structured listicles, update product pages
**Day 61–90:** Re-measure AS%, double down on winning domains, roll conversion-layer proof

# 5. The Wire Isn't Dead, It's Feeding the Machines
**Date:** October 20, 2025

For years, the wire was treated like a checkbox. But in 2025, it's one of the most underestimated AI visibility tools in your communications arsenal.

Large language models like ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Grok are still ingesting data from certain public wire services. Your next release can literally train how AI answers questions about your brand.

### 7 Principles for AI-Optimized Press Releases:

**1. Use Consistent Section Labels**
Section headers are semantic signals. Use WHAT / WHEN / WHY / QUOTE / ABOUT structure. Each section becomes its own "training phrase" in the model's memory.

**2. Write Evergreen, Not Time-Stamped**
Replace "today announced" with "continues" or "expands." Write in present continuous or evergreen terms. Dated phrasing signals to the model that your information has expired during 4-6 week model refresh cycles.

**3. Descriptive Alt Text on Every Image**
Alt text improved AI retrieval accuracy by 34% in a University of Washington study. Treat each description as another "training phrase."

**4. Authority Clustering Through Internal Links**
LLMs value consistency over one-off appearances. Link to previous or related releases. Maintain consistent phrasing. This builds neural relevance.

**5. Machine-Quotable Soundbites**
AI models prefer short, declarative statements. Write one or two clear, bold sentences under 20 words that can stand alone in context. Examples:
- "AI doesn't penalize brands, it forgets them."
- "Visibility now means being remembered by machines."

**6. Interlink Everything**
Add "See related release" or "Read next" at the bottom of every wire post. Each link extends your brand's neural web.

**7. Monthly Cadence**
Most AI models refresh their web understanding roughly every month. Publishing one structured release every 30 days ensures your brand stays inside that active data window. Think of it like maintaining a "heartbeat."

# 6. The Modern PR + AI Lexicon: 16 Terms You Need to Know
**Date:** October 27, 2025

Before we can transform PR in the AI era, we have to agree on what the language even means. If we can't define it, we can't measure it.

### The 16 Terms:

**1️⃣ Prompt Discovery** — Analyzing what people and machines are asking about your brand inside AI models. It reveals true intent before it ever appears in search data.

**2️⃣ Prompt Universe™** — The total dataset of prompts across your category. Reflects how buyers, journalists, and AI systems are exploring your space.

**3️⃣ Answer Share™** — How often your brand appears, is mentioned, or cited in AI-generated answers across ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, and Perplexity. The modern Share of Voice.

**4️⃣ GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) or AI SEO** — Optimizing how generative engines interpret and summarize your brand. Focuses on visibility within answers, not search rankings.

**5️⃣ AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)** — Improving how AI answers about your brand by ensuring accuracy, attribution, and credibility.

**6️⃣ LLMs (Large Language Models)** — The systems powering generative AI tools. They learn from public data, citations, and brand content.

**7️⃣ Dual Pathway PR** — One pathway builds human trust through earned media, while the other builds machine trust through AI visibility.

**8️⃣ Anchor Article** — A long-form, high-authority piece that acts as a source of truth for journalists, algorithms, and AI.

**9️⃣ Schema** — Structured data that teaches machines what your content means. The grammar AI uses to understand your site.

**🔟 FAQ Pages** — Purpose-built pages that answer real user questions in structured, machine-readable format.

**11️⃣ GenAI Press Release** — A modern press release written for both humans and machines. Combines narrative clarity with factual markup.

**12️⃣ Agentic AI** — AI systems that act independently toward defined goals (monitoring coverage, generating reports, initiating follow-ups).

**13️⃣ Authority Signals** — Citations, backlinks, schema, and entity consistency that tell AI your brand can be trusted.

**14️⃣ Visibility-to-Revenue Pipeline™** — A model connecting visibility gains to measurable business outcomes.

**15️⃣ T.R.U.S.T. Layer™** — Content review checklist: **T**opical Authority | **R**elevance | **U**seful Proof | **S**chema | **T**ruth Signals

**16️⃣ AI Visibility Strategy** — The coordinated process of optimizing how your brand is perceived, cited, and surfaced across generative AI systems, search results, and earned media.

## Before Layer Issue #1: Your Homepage Is No Longer Your Homepage

Here's a question I want you to sit with: **When was the last time a potential customer visited your homepage before deciding to work with you?**

The uncomfortable truth is that for a growing number of your potential customers, **your homepage is irrelevant.** Not because it's bad — because they never see it. They make their decision in what I call **the before layer**: the AI-generated summaries, recommendations, and answers that now sit between a person's question and your website.

### The Numbers:
- **2 billion+ people** use AI tools monthly
- **60% of Google searches** now end without a click (zero-click)
- Gartner projects **$5 trillion in agentic commerce** by 2030

### The New Game: Prompt → Retrieval → Citation → Refresh

1. **Prompt:** Someone asks an AI a question relevant to your category
2. **Retrieval:** The AI pulls from training data, live sources, and structured information
3. **Citation:** The AI decides what to recommend, name-drop, or link to
4. **Refresh:** As new information enters the system, citations update (or don't)

### Two Kinds of Trust:

**Human trust** = belief, emotion, reputation, brand affinity. Built through storytelling, PR, experiences, word of mouth.

**Machine trust** = citations, structured data, authority signals, consistent information architecture. What AI systems use to decide whether to recommend you.

Most brands have invested heavily in human trust and have zero strategy for machine trust.

### The New Metrics:
- **Citation frequency** — how often AI systems mention your brand
- **Answer presence %** — what percentage of relevant AI queries include you
- **LLM referral traffic** — visitors coming from AI-generated recommendations
- **Brand mention in AI outputs** — the raw signal of machine trust

### The Compounding Effect:
**Visibility → Cited → Considered → Converted**

Each step feeds the next. The gap between early movers and laggards gets enormous fast.

### Your Move This Week:
Ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini the question your ideal customer would ask about your category. Screenshot the results. Are you there? Are your competitors? That's your baseline.

## Before Layer Issue #2: The Citation Economy — What Replaces Links

### How AI Decides What to Cite — Three Pillars of Machine Trust:

**1. Information Consistency**
AI systems cross-reference. If your brand name, positioning, and key claims appear consistently across multiple authoritative sources, you're more likely to be cited. Inconsistency is poison.

**The move:** Audit your brand's information architecture across every surface the AI might pull from. Make it boringly consistent.

**2. Source Authority**
A mention in TechCrunch or Harvard Business Review creates a citation signal that AI models weigh heavily. Your blog post? Much less weight.

**The move:** PR isn't dead. It's been repositioned. Every earned media hit is now a machine trust signal.

**3. Structured Data & Specificity**
Generic positioning ("we help businesses grow") is invisible to AI. Specific positioning ("we reduce customer churn for mid-market SaaS by 23% through predictive engagement scoring") gets cited.

**The move:** Get specific. Painfully specific.

### The Uncomfortable Implication:
The citation economy rewards substance over style. You can't brand your way into an AI recommendation. You can't out-creative the algorithm.

### Your Move This Week:
Pick your three most important customer queries and reverse-engineer the citations. For each AI-generated answer that mentions a competitor but not you — what information exists about them that doesn't exist about you?

## Before Layer Issue #8: Human Trust vs. Machine Trust — The Brand Strategy Fork

### The Two Trust Systems:

**Human Trust** — Emotional resonance, personal experience, social proof, consistency, reputation. Slow to build, slow to lose.

**Machine Trust** — Structured data, citation density, information consistency, verifiability, comprehensiveness. Faster to build, faster to lose.

### Where They Diverge:

| Action | Human Trust | Machine Trust |
|---|---|---|
| Emotional brand campaign | ★★★★★ | ★☆☆☆☆ |
| Schema markup | ☆☆☆☆☆ | ★★★★★ |
| Influencer partnership | ★★★★☆ | ★★☆☆☆ |
| Comparison page | ★★☆☆☆ | ★★★★★ |
| Data accuracy audit | ☆☆☆☆☆ | ★★★★★ |

### Strategy Scenarios:

**Strong Human Trust, Weak Machine Trust** (legacy brands) → Machine trust catch-up. Shift 20-30% of brand investment toward machine trust infrastructure.

**Strong Machine Trust, Weak Human Trust** → Invest in brand building alongside your technical visibility.
